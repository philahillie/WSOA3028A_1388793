HOW DOES "THE MEDIUM IS THE MESSAGE" IMPACT OUR UNDERSTANDING OF THE WEB?

26 April 2021

"The Medium is the Message" highlights how the meaning of an artwork or content is in the medium or platform that it has been shared through as opposed to the content shared. When thinking of the web and the various platforms that are available for one to share content on, one cannot ignore the fact that different digital platforms cater to specific content and thus aid in the dissemination of said content.

Marshall McLuhan discusses how a medium can "change the scale and/or pace" of content and how it is introduced to us and says that the focus should be on what one does with the machine as opposed to the machine itself. When I think of how this theory could be applied in everyday life, I think about the various digital platforms that exist and how we are subconsiously aware of which platforms serve the varying content we wish to share. When we have visual content we want to share, we post on Instagram; when we have thoughts we wnat to share, we tweet them on Twitter and when content creators want to share videos longer than a minute, they look into sharing those videos on YouTube. These digital platforms can be used for functions outside of what has been mentioned but there is a chance that the content will be received differently and will have less impact.

McLuhan mentions how the electric light can be seen as a medium and the objects or activities it illuminates can be seen as the content. He discusses how the content is often seen as the medium as it is at the forefront but the content cannot exist without the medium the same way a brain surgery is not possible without the lights that help the surgeons see what they are doing. Similarly, the web is the medium beacuse without it, content as we know it today, cannot be shared or accessed instantly.

As a multimedia artist who is trying to share her work on a digital platform, I have debated if there is value in creating a personal website to share my work when there are existing "free" digital platforms with millions of users and account holders. One of the benefits that first come to mind is the ability to display y work in the format I wish instead of having to conform to the grid structure of Instagram. While Instagram is a valuable platform for gaining followers, a website could be a valuable platform for creating a more personal voice and image. I believe that my Instagram account could breathe life into my website and they could exist as two complimentary spaces.

My artwork and thoughts are the content and my medium is the Web. The web is a digital space in which information is stores and shared instantly and my content is being stored on Instagram and this website, both of which are digital spaces that act as archives.
