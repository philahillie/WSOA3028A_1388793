A REFLECTION ON THE SET READINGS

3 May 2021

by Phila Hillie

The content I have read on the web, its conception and the tools that help it run efficiently have been interesting because most of them were written before the World Wide Web was launch and are abstract in style. For example, the idea on which thw web was founded on was to be a source of information and arcive knowledge at volumes that the human brain could not. This idea is similar to that of the library and thus redaings such as "The Library of Babel" help explain the concept of the web in a way that is understandable by people who are not just in the computer science community. In fact, the conceptualisation of a space in which a large amount of information could be stored and live longer than humans was initally rejected by scientists and encouraged by philosophers and librarians such as Jorges Luis Borges.

In "The Garden of Forking Paths", Borges writes about Dr Ts'ui Pen, a man whose ideas around creating a space in which possible outcomes are endless and thus a story can have multiple endings were not taken seriously by his family. His family wished to discard his findings when he died but the publishers and people from different cultural backgrounds and locations saw value in his work. I feel that this reading speaks to the fact that the web is now full of a large volume of information, some of which may be useful to some and not useful to others. I think this point ties in with the idea of metadata, a tool that helps users access information that is useful to them. This also has ties with hypertext which is "human-authored media that can branch or perform on request". Hypertext works on the idea that you can create associations between media that can lead you to more information about the media that you were previously reviewing. Hypertext responds to human actions the same way the stories and their potential endings respond to the requests of the reader.

One of the other recurring themes of the set readings is the idea of the web being referred to as an artwork or medium which content can be shared on. Marshall McLuhan made the statement that "The Medium is the Message" and this can influence the way information or content is viewed and/or shared. Dr Ts'ui Pen's work was not regarded as useful to his family because it had been presented in what looked like loose pages of thoghts that wre not cohesive or complete however, the Englishamn who read the book on his ideas was able to understand the idea that was being communicated. McLuhan stated that the "message of any medium or technology is the scale or pace or pattern that is introduced into human affairs" and this can be seen in the reception of Dr Pen's work before and after publication.

When I intially thought of the web, I thought of technology and systems that could not be understood by people who were not in that profession. Seeing the early conceptualisation of the web in abstract and fictional stories and essays has humanised the web and highlighted the one main goal of the web; to act as an archive for information that can live longer than humans.
