ACCESSIBILITY & USABILITY ON THE WEB

Accessibility on the web speaks to "the discriminatory aspects related to the user experience for people with disabilities". Usability on the web speaks to "designing products that are efficient and satisfying to users". Unfortunately, when websites are designed, accessibility and usability are not regarded as integral to the initial development of websites and are forced into the designed website instead of accommodated for from the beginning of the design process. Accessibility addresses making websites that can be engaged with by people who have physical and cognitive disabilities.

When researching the suggestions that forums such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0 (WCAG) had recommended, my mind was suddenly exposed to design considerations that my privilege had shielded me from. For example, there are fonts that are being developed that will make it easier for people with dyslexia to read. I was also made aware of how important consistency and hierarchy in the layout of web pages is. This includes a consistent navigation menu in which the page links are placed in the same order on every page.

It is important for people with disabilities to be involved in the design process so they are able to give valuable input into what needs to be on the website in order for it to be as accessible as possible. People with disabilities should be able to access your website with or without Assistive Technology. The WCAG consists of a set of guidelines and rules for creating accessible web pages. These guidelines show us how to consider accessibility in web pages regarding a range of disabilities like physical, visual, auditory, speech, language, cognitive and neurological disabilities.

While accessibility can be addressed graphically and in the user experience on the user's side, it can also be incorporated into the technical design of the web pages through the use of metadata. This includes adding descriptions to the visuals you are using and adding microformats such as an h-card that will allow for your website and information about it to appear easily when people search for it on browsers. Usability addresses how one can easily consume the content on your website in the most efficient way.

The Easy-to-Read (E2R) is a method that looks into writing text in a form that is easy to read and easy to understand. The involvement of users in the design process is vital and the target groups give reviews. An efficient layout will be one with consistent navigation, clear and short sentences with elaborated text and a clear separation between the content and navigation. The presence of a search function that is able to support the location of single words or articles is encouraged. Medium-sized font that is scalable is preferred over small font and the font should be clear and bold. This is why websites tend to have a sans-serif font. Pictures and patterns that clash with the background and text should also be avoided.

My website has a focus on visuals as it showcases my art and the art of people I admire. Whilst there is theory and text, I have decided to keep it minimal and lay out the visuals and text in a way that does not clash or confuse viewers. The website is also very clear in its colour palette and mainly uses black and white to help with contrast and visibility.
