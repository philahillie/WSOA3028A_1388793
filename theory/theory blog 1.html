A REFLECTION ON THE READINGS & HOW THEY RELATE TO THE IDEAS & IDEALS OF THE INTERNET

16 April 2021

by Phila Hillie

Jorges Luis Borges was a librarian whose explorations of infinite sources of information and possibilities that can transcend time and space can be seen as an early conceptualisation of the World Wide Web and the Internet.

In "The Library of Babel", Borges describes a world which is made up of interconnected, hexagonal libraries whose boundaries cannot be defined. Each library is made up of four sides that contain twenty bookshelves. He describes how eah book is different from the other and that the difference in titles could be distinguished by a single letter or punctuation mark. The titles may or may not describe the contents of the books and this is what makes them significant to the history of man. Borges states that the books aim to solve "riddles" that were once unsolvable by man but cannot solve and provide answers for everything.

This piece of writing describes the idea on which the internet was founded on; to serve as a historic archive and organise human knowledge. The internet was an exploration of how poeple think and communicate. Borges describes the center of the libraries as hexagonal in shape and this is consistent with the desriptions of the internet as non-linear. The interent is a "global system of interconnected computer networks" similar to the interconnected hexagonal libraries. They both combine the thoughts of millions and puts them in one boundless place.

"The Garden of Forking Paths" is a short story about an Asian man who finds an Englishman who has been studying his late grandfather's work. His grandfather, Dr Ts'ui pen, spent the last years of his life writing a novel that had an infinite number of possibilities. His family asumed the novel had not been completed as they could not understand that the reader could choose the fate of the characters in the novel. The novel was about a labyrinth that was assumed to not have been completed by his grandson as he could not navigate or make sense of the map. His grandson mentioned how surprised he was that the work of his Chinese grandfather was being studied and understood by the Englishman.

The internet blurs national and international barriers and this can be seen in its accessibility and its ability to be translated into multiple languages. Information can also be shared by people across the world and that information then lives on the internet.

"As We May Think" discusses using computers to process information, the hypertext, the memex and the web. Tim Burners-Lee, the creator of the World Wide Web, brought together the elctronic document, hypertext, markup and the internet to create a new paradigm. Bush expressed how a mechanised library could solve problems with the amount of time it took to access information and he rate of production of information. The mcahnised library would increase searching speed and allow for more knowledge to be acquired by viewers. Bush refers to this device as a memex. The information would be able to be stored at a greater volume and life span than that of humans.
