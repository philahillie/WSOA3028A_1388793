CRITICAL ANALYSIS OF UX & UI IN MY WEBSITE

Posted 14 June 2021 by Phila Hillie

User Experience (UX) can be described as the purpose and functionality of the product and User Interface (UI) can be described as the design attributes that determine how easy a website is to engage with and how well a website performs. UI can include specific assets that users interact with such as colours, images and typography.

When a website is designed with good UX, it reduces complaints from customers and increases their trust in your brand. The UX design of a website is typically done first. When I think of my website, the first thing that comes to mind is a consistent and clear navigation menu and the structure of the website. My website relies more on visuals therefore I have incorporated elements such as an image slideshow that will be present on the home page, about page and blog pages that will showcase the images created by Milani Media House. This reminds the viewer of what the product that is being promoted is. This is my way of including visual elements in the structural make-up of the website. I am also working on creating an immersive gallery page in which people can feel as though they have access to me work on a more intimate level. As of now, I will achieve this through large 3D drawings that are from the perspective of the viewer but a long-term goal of mine is to be able to embed videos of an exhibition space (real or virtual with the help of 3D programs such as SketchUp).

With regards to the UI, my website showcases my artistic abilities and experiences in Johannesburg through exhibitions and photography of my creative process. The colour palette of my website is black and white with accents of bright red and a clay orange colour. The presence of the red represents confidence and passion while the lighter orange colour can be seen as a reference to my African context. I am still working on integrating more responsive design tools into my website such as making my website accessible on desktops and mobile phones. I want to be able to create an experience in which users can engage with me and my work and feel like they are part of my creative process and audience. I would love to eventually also incorporate a comment section so that I can communicate with the audience and they can feel like they have a degree of access to me.
