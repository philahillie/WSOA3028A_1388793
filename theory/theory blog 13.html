CRITICAL ANALYSIS OF UX & UI IN MY WEBSITE

14 June 2021

by Phila Hillie

User Experience (UX) can be described as the purpose and functionality of the product and User Interface (UI) can be described as the design attributes that determine how easy a website is to engage with and how well a website performs. UI can include specific assets that users interact with such as colours, images and typography.

The difference between UX and UI is not always clear so an analogy that was made by Alan Smith helped me break it down and understand it. Essentially - when looking at a house - the physical structure, plumbing electrical system and HVAC are the functionality and thus the UX of the house. The finishes such as the windows hardware, doorknobs, faucets and paint are how the homeowner interacts with the house and their enjoyment of it and this is the UI. Therefore, when applied to the website context, UX can be seen as the coding that helps the website run efficiently and the wireframes that help plan where functions will be placed and UI can be achieved through the style guides that determine colour schemes, animation, imagery, buttons and typography and overall visual effects t hat will keep the user engaged and help them enjoy the products more.

The benefits of UX include design that is centred around the user and sees value in creating elements that allow users to have a positive e xperience with the product. It looks at creating a context in which users can experience the website and how it showcases those products in the most satisfactory and efficient way possible. When a website is designed with good UX, it reduces complaints from customers and increases their trust in your brand. The UX design of a website is typically done first. To put it simply, UX acts as the "organs" of the website and makes interfaces or the products that you interact with useful and functional. When I think of my website, the first thing that comes to mind is a consistent and clear navigation menu. I have also decided to put my recent posts on the home page for ease of navigation and so that returning users can quickly access the most recent information instead of having to scroll through all they content they have already consumed. Using good semantic markup ensures that that code reads easily and thus is easy for developers to engage with and add more content to. Using a consistent layout that is readable across all the pages on your website (i.e., the about page, contact page and blog pages) will also increase the users’ likeliness to return to your site.

UI looks at the aesthetic and interactive aspects of the website. It l ooks into how the product is presented to users and how users can interact with the product. This means that – on a website – one would have to integrate interactive mechanisms in order for the customer to feel like they are part of the experience. An interactive mechanism could be a button that momentarily changes colour when clicked on, a drop-down menu, articles that pop out when the mouse hovers over them and websites that are equally efficient and aesthetically pleasing on mobile phones. Imagery helps the user see what the website represents or is trying to sell to them and typography that is clean and readable but minimal in text helps the user read easily but not lose interest due to the presence of long sentences.

My website is image based as it showcases my artistic abilities and experiences in Johannesburg through exhibitions and photography of my creative process. The colour palette of my website is black and white with accents of bright red and a clay orange colour. The presence of the red is intended to be interpreted as confidence and passion while the lighter orange colour can be seen as a reference to my African context. I am still working on integrating more responsive design tools into my website such as buttons and a gallery page in which users can immerse themselves into my work from the comfort of their own homes. I want to be able to create an experience in which users can engage with me and my work and feel like they are part of my creative process and audience. I would love to eventually also incorporate a comment section so that I can communicate with the audience and they can feel like they have a degree of access to me.
