RELECTIONS ON READINGS BY JORGE LUIS BORGES
Jorge Luis Borges was an Argentine librarian whose explorations of infintite sourcs of information, history and possibilities that can transcend time and space can be sen an an early conceptualisation of the World Wide Web and the Internet.
THE PROMISE & PERIL OF USER-GENERATED CONTENT User-generated content can be defined as data, information or media that is contributed to the web by ordinary people. In recent years, user-generated content has seen rapid growth and an increase in value as it entertains its consumers, is usually relatable in its nature and is easily accessible to its users.
A REFLECTION ON WARDRIP-FRUIN "WHAT HYPERTEXT IS"

The term "hypertext" was first coined by Theodor "Ted" Nelson and was describd as human-authored media that can branch or perform on request. The concept of the hypertext has varying definitions for different fields and has its own history outside of a Web context.

THE MEDIUM IS THE MESSAGE "The Medium is the Message" highlights how the meaning of an artwork or content is in the medium or platform that it has been shared through as opposed to the content shared. When thinking of the web and the various platforms that are available for one to share content on, one cannot ignore the fact that different digital platforms cater to specific content and thus aid in the dissemination of said content.
THE USES OF METADATA - WHAT, WHY AND HOW? Metadata can be defined and abstracted into several meanings but, at its core, it is information about other information. Metadata describes and gives more information about an object.
