A REFLECTION ON WARDRIP-FRUIN "WHAT HYPERTEXT IS"

23 April 2021

by Phila Hillie

The term "hypertext" was first coined by Theodor "Ted" Nelson and was described as human-authored data that can branch or perform on request. The concept of the ypertext has varying definitions for different fields and has its own history outside of a Web context.

In the literary community, hypertext can be considered a direct connection from one position in a text to another. This definition mentions the idea of a link and this is the first one that came to my mind; the act of linking two web pages to one another through a word, phrase or image. Early iterations of the hypertext looked at how words in books could physically be linked to another word that is several pages forward or backwards.

In the computer science community, hypertext can be defined as hypertext-as-interaction with information to uild associations and through associations to build knowledge. This definition reminds me of Jorge Borges' "The Library of Babel" and how the hexagonal libraries were interconnected. It reminds about the principles that the web was founded on which is to store knowledge. This means that hypertext aids in the building of knowledge. While the defintion does not mention a link, the 'associations' used to build knowledge can be seen as links and a way of connecting one source of knowledge to another.

Differing intellectual communities have their own interpretations of hypertext because the name 'hypertext' is simply a word that was theorised by Ted Nelson. This means that it did not have a defintive meaning before Nelson used it in his research paper. If one wants to truly understand what hypertext is, one has to understand what it means to different intellectual communities and also understand its history.

I understand hypertext to be a function that lead you to more information about the media or text that it has been attached to. For example, in my website, I have attached previews of my blog posts with hypertext or hypermedia 'links' to lead you to the full article. This is my way of associating two piece if information to one another and giving the viewer a choice on whether they would want more information the opportunity to spend less time on information they are not interested in and more time to find something else they might like. Hypertext can also be seen as a tool that helps with navigation as the viewer can be brought forwards and backwards to where they were.

I want my website to have a focus on visual so I would love to make use of hypermedia and hypertext to be able to show visuals but allow viewers to learn more about the visuals through hypertext. I believe that the visual associates itself with the text in a hierarchal structure.
