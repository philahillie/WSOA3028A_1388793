DIGITAL INEQUALITIES

31 May 2021

Digital Inequalities can be seen as the differences in structures that are put in place to give people access to the internet and technology. They are reflective of the existing socioeconomic, infrastructure and education environments of underprivileged communities and present challenges that can affect all aspects of one's life from being able to work remotely to being able to engage with social media platforms

Christoph Lutz discusses how there are three levels of divides present and explains how they affect people and how they could be improved. The first divide looks at the unequal access to the internet. There is a lack of theory-based research into how the internet can be accessed and run efficiently in different ways or forms other than the computer. For example, many people do not have access to a computer but can access the Internet through their mobile devices. However, due to the lack of theoretical research, a focus has not been placed on creating websites that can be engaged with efficiently on the phone. While cellular banking and certain online shops have accommodated their users, a focus has been placed on making social media accessible on phones rather and thus other traditional sites that are not flexible or consume a large amount of data on phones.
